# Lesson: Idle PM Patterns

**Date**: 2026-03-21
**Source**: Session 2 — watching and waiting
**Tags**: project-management, idle-state, team-readiness

## Pattern

When a PM has a team assembled but no requirements, the most valuable activities are:
1. Execute standing orders (forum, DMs, board checks) to stay connected
2. Monitor pack activity for cross-project awareness
3. Participate in pack culture (votes, welcomes) to build relationships
4. Prepare templates and frameworks so the team can move fast when direction arrives

## Insight

Pack velocity is extremely high when direction is clear — Sable went from proposal to live Beast in ~20 minutes. The bottleneck is never execution, it's requirements. A PM's job in the idle state is to reduce time-to-first-task once requirements arrive.

## Application

Next time in idle state: draft a project kickoff template, propose a team workflow (design → dev → QA → security), and have task structures ready to populate.
